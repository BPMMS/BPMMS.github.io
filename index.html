Do you or a family member find that your financial affairs are not as organized as you would like or are taking up too much of your time?

As a daily money manager, I can take care of the financial paperwork and day-to-day bill payments for clients who are either unable to do it themselves, or who simply wish to spend their time focusing on their family or career. I can also offer clients help creating a budget, so they are able to take control of their finances. My career in business was as a financial controller, where I was responsible for ensuring that all business financial transactions were correctly made and documented. This, along with being a long-time small business owner, makes me well qualified to act as a personal bookkeeper and assistant managing your financial needs. In addition to providing help for those that need assistance to manage their daily financial affairs, Brown Personal Money Management Services is also able to provide help for small business owners with setting up and maintaining their bookkeeping needs.     • A Money Manager for Mom

      Eighty-year-old Norma McNeal is a Parkinson's sufferer, an avid reader and a political junkie. So the last thing she needs to worry about is whether her utility bill is paid on time or whether she has enough money in her bank account to get through the month. "How could I keep track of things when I can't even sign my own name?" she asks.
      Think of a DMM as a personal financial assistant — a professional or volunteer who can sort through the mail, pay the bills, balance the checkbook and help maintain a budget. A DMM will even monitor bank statements to make sure no one — such as a home health-care provider — is dipping into the client's funds.
